Laura Scholz-Díaz

I am a 1st. year PhD student at the Instituto de Astrofísica de Canarias (IAC). I am working in the field of galaxy formation and evolution, and I am currently studying nearby galaxies by looking into their stellar population properties. 

Particularly, I am interested in the connection between galaxies and their host dark matter haloes, galaxy quenching, and also in the imprint that super-massive black holes leave in their host galaxies. 

Credit: ESA/Hubble, L. Calçada (ESO)

Although I am currently working with optical spectra from galaxies in the local Universe, during my master's thesis I also studied the origin of the gas fuelling star formation in simulated galaxies from the EAGLE project.
